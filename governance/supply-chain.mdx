## Supply chain frameworks

### I. EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD)

**Status:** ‚úÖ Enforced  
**Region:** üá™üá∫ European Union  
**Scope:** Human rights & environmental due diligence

**Requirements:**
- Identify adverse impacts in supply chain
- Prevent, mitigate, or remediate impacts
- Establish grievance mechanism
- Monitor effectiveness of measures

### II. German Supply Chain Due Diligence Act (LkSG)

**Status:** ‚úÖ Enforced  
**Region:** üá©üá™ Germany  
**Scope:** Human rights & environmental protection

**Requirements:**
- Risk analysis of direct suppliers
- Grievance mechanism for affected parties
- Annual reporting on due diligence
- Remedial action for violations

### III. Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act (UFLPA)

**Status:** ‚úÖ Enforced  
**Region:** üá∫üá∏ United States  
**Scope:** Forced labor prevention

**Requirements:**
- Rebuttable presumption for XUAR goods
- Supply chain traceability
- Due diligence documentation
- Import blocking for non-compliant goods

### IV. Dodd-Frank ¬ß1502 (Conflict Minerals)

**Status:** ‚úÖ Enforced  
**Region:** üá∫üá∏ United States  
**Scope:** 3TG minerals (Tin, Tantalum, Tungsten, Gold)

**Requirements:**
- Reasonable country of origin inquiry (RCOI)
- Due diligence on source and chain of custody
- Independent private sector audit
- Annual disclosure to SEC

## Compliance engine

from governance_kernel.compliance_matrix import ComplianceMatrix, SectoralContext

matrix = ComplianceMatrix()

# Check component origin
result = matrix.check_sectoral_compliance(

## Bill-of-Materials (BOM) ledger

iLuminara maintains a cryptographic Bill-of-Materials ledger for hardware attestation:

```python
from governance_kernel.bom_ledger import BOMLedger

ledger = BOMLedger()

# Register hardware component
ledger.register_component(
    component_id="JETSON_ORIN_001",
    component_type="Edge Device",
    manufacturer="NVIDIA",
    origin_country="Taiwan",
    materials=["Silicon", "Copper", "Gold"],
    supplier_audit={
        "auditor": "Bureau Veritas",
        "date": "2024-12-01",
        "result": "COMPLIANT"
    },
    conflict_minerals_status="CONFLICT_FREE"
)

# Verify component compliance
compliance = ledger.verify_component("JETSON_ORIN_001")
print(f"Compliance status: {compliance['status']}")
```

## Grievance mechanism

### CSDDD & LkSG requirement

Both CSDDD and LkSG require an accessible grievance mechanism for affected parties:

```python
from governance_kernel.grievance_mechanism import GrievanceMechanism

mechanism = GrievanceMechanism()

# Submit grievance
grievance_id = mechanism.submit_grievance(
    reporter={
        "type": "supplier_worker",
        "location": "Kenya",
        "anonymous": True
    },
    complaint={
        "category": "working_conditions",
        "description": "Unsafe working conditions at supplier facility",
        "evidence": ["photo_001.jpg", "photo_002.jpg"]
    }
)

# Track grievance status
status = mechanism.get_grievance_status(grievance_id)
print(f"Status: {status['current_status']}")
print(f"Assigned to: {status['assigned_investigator']}")
```

### Grievance workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Submission">
    Affected party submits grievance (anonymous or identified)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acknowledgment">
    Automatic acknowledgment within 24 hours
  </Step>
  <Step title="Investigation">
    Assigned investigator conducts due diligence
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remediation">
    Corrective action plan developed and implemented
  </Step>
  <Step title="Closure">
    Grievance resolved and documented in audit trail
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supply chain risk assessment

### Automated risk scoring

```python
from governance_kernel.supply_chain_risk import SupplyChainRiskAssessor

assessor = SupplyChainRiskAssessor()

# Assess supplier risk
risk_score = assessor.assess_supplier(
    supplier_id="SUPPLIER_001",
    location="Democratic Republic of Congo",
    materials=["Tantalum", "Tin"],
    audit_history=[
        {"date": "2024-06-01", "result": "COMPLIANT"},
        {"date": "2024-12-01", "result": "COMPLIANT"}
    ],
    grievances_count=0
)

print(f"Risk score: {risk_score['score']}/100")
print(f"Risk level: {risk_score['level']}")  # LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, CRITICAL
```

### Risk factors

| Factor | Weight | Description |
|--------|--------|-------------|
| **Geographic risk** | 30% | Country-level human rights & conflict risk |
| **Material risk** | 25% | Conflict minerals, forced labor materials |
| **Audit history** | 20% | Frequency and results of audits |
| **Grievance history** | 15% | Number and severity of grievances |
| **Transparency** | 10% | Supply chain visibility and documentation |

generator = SECDisclosureGenerator()

## Monitoring & alerts

### Real-time supply chain monitoring

```python
from governance_kernel.supply_chain_monitor import SupplyChainMonitor

monitor = SupplyChainMonitor()

# Monitor for violations
monitor.start_monitoring(
    suppliers=["SUPPLIER_001", "SUPPLIER_002"],
    alert_on=[
        "UFLPA_VIOLATION",
        "CONFLICT_MINERALS_DETECTED",
        "GRIEVANCE_SUBMITTED",
        "AUDIT_FAILED"
    ]
)

# Receive alerts
@monitor.on_alert
def handle_alert(alert):
    print(f"üö® {alert['type']}: {alert['message']}")
    print(f"   Supplier: {alert['supplier_id']}")
    print(f"   Action required: {alert['action']}")
```
