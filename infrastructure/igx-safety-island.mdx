## Overview

The Hardware Root-of-Trust (HRoT) provides **physical certainty** that iLuminara's compliance guardrails cannot be bypassed. While Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) offer software-defined security, the IGX Safety Island adds a hardware-level kill switch that makes it physically impossible to violate the 47-framework compliance architecture.

The NVIDIA IGX Orin Safety Microcontroller (sMCU) creates an isolated "Safety Island" that monitors AI inference operations and physically cuts power if rogue actors attempt to compromise the system.

## The certainty gap

### Software-defined security (TEE)
- Vulnerable to sophisticated software exploits
- Relies on cryptographic attestation
- Can be compromised by zero-day vulnerabilities

### Hardware root-of-trust (IGX Safety Island)
- Physical isolation from main compute
- IEC 61508 SIL-2 certification path
- Impossible to bypass without physical access
- Autonomous power control

The sMCU continuously monitors:
1. **Compliance violations** — Any attempt to bypass the 47-framework guardrails
